### Content Insights

- Nice to see all of the PHP features - echo, loops, functions, includes - working together in a single file and how fast it is to get something running.
- PHP makes sense for small-scale, fast development where the added complexity of a framework like React genuinely isn't worth it.
- PHP feels less elegant than React to me because of having to break out of HTML with `<?php` tags. In React you have JSX - you write a component and drop it in like `<LoginPage />`. That mental model feels cleaner.
- Php has not died and is in fact thriving, still evolving. the statement of it dying may be thrown around because its boring, proven, reliable, and not the flashy forefront. holds 77% market share of full scale staff augmentation. Its the statistics that bring php to the top, mature ecosystems, lots of talent etc, decades of production tested libraries.

### Career/Employability/Learning Insights

- language choice in professional environments is rarely about elegance, it's about risk and cost. This is the delta between what developers prefer and what business choose, in the workforce important to understand why this gap matters.
- My process was to start building, hit a problem, then ask AI to point me to the right place in W3Schools rather than reading docs upfront. That loop worked well and kept momentum. Decided to build from scratch rather than fork the example repo - that choice forced me to actually understand the syntax rather than just modify existing code.
